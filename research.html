Research Overview

Understanding how performance-based compensation transforms educational organizations through systematic capacity building rather than individual incentives.

"How does the Teacher Incentive Allotment affect school organizational capacity and through what mechanisms do these effects operate to influence student outcomes?"

Critical Literature Gaps Addressed

  • Measurement Gap: No validated measure of organizational capacity exists using administrative data
  • Mechanism Gap: How performance-based compensation actually works through organizational structures remains unclear
  • Equity Gap: Differential effects across student populations and institutional contexts underexplored
  • Temporal Gap: Long-term organizational development processes vs. immediate policy effects

Three-Paper Dissertation Framework

1
Development & Validation of Campus Readiness Index
Establishes CRI as a valid measure of organizational capacity using factor analysis and latent class modeling
2
Examining Outcomes Across Contexts
Investigates differential effects across student populations and institutional contexts
3
Policy Impacts on CRI
Analyzes how TIA affects organizational capacity through causal inference methods
Integration: Each paper builds systematically to answer how performance-based compensation transforms schools through organizational capacity, revealing both the mechanisms and conditions for effectiveness.

Expected Contributions

Four major anticipated discoveries that will reshape our understanding of how performance-based compensation works in education.

Contribution 1: TIA Effects Will Vary by Institutional Context

Hypothesis: Differential Implementation
Expected Finding
  • Traditional Public Schools: Anticipated to demonstrate stronger positive responses to TIA due to standardized institutional structures
  • Charter Schools: Expected to show more variable organizational capacity improvements due to diverse governance models
  • Rural vs Urban: Hypothesized similar effectiveness across contexts despite different resource constraints
  • Equity-Neutral Policy: Expected consistent effects across high/low poverty schools

Contribution 2: Organizational Changes Will Require Time

Hypothesis: Temporal Development
Expected Finding
  • Year 0-1: Anticipated minimal immediate effects as organizations adapt
  • Year 2: Expected peak improvements as new practices become embedded
  • Year 3+: Projected sustained moderate effects as capacity stabilizes
  • Key Insight: Organizations will need 2-3 years to fully adapt to performance incentives

Contribution 3: Organizational Capacity as Critical Mediator

Hypothesis: Mediation Analysis
Preliminary Analysis Available
  • TIA → CRI Effect: Anticipated positive relationship with cumulative exposure
  • CRI → Achievement: Expected strong relationship across mathematics and reading
  • Indirect Effect (ACME): Predicted to be primary pathway for TIA effectiveness
  • Direct Effect: Anticipated to be minimal - organizational capacity as the mechanism

Contribution 4: Greatest Benefits for Historically Marginalized Students

Hypothesis: Educational Equity
Initial Patterns Observed
  • Emergent Bilingual Students: Expected to show highest benefits from organizational capacity
  • Special Education Students: Anticipated significant advantages in high-capacity schools
  • Economically Disadvantaged: Projected substantial capacity-related improvements
  • Key Insight: High-capacity schools will provide greatest benefits to historically marginalized populations

Methodology

A comprehensive analytical strategy combining multiple methodological approaches to establish causal relationships and validate theoretical propositions.

Key Methodological Innovation

Campus Readiness Index (CRI): First validated measure of organizational capacity using administrative data, enabling systematic assessment across large numbers of schools without resource-intensive primary data collection.

Three-Paper Analytical Approach:
  • Paper 1: Factor analysis, latent class analysis, criterion validity
  • Paper 2: Fixed effects regression with interaction terms across contexts
  • Paper 3: Event study design, difference-in-differences, mediation analysis

Policy Implications

Actionable insights for policymakers, district leaders, researchers, and practitioners based on expected research outcomes.

Expected Policy Impact

This research will provide evidence-based guidance on how organizational capacity investments can advance educational equity, directly informing federal discussions about teacher quality initiatives and Title I funding mechanisms.
